Recently, I had the opportunity to design the lighting for a short, one-act play
called "The Proposal” written by Anton Chekhov. It was an amazing experience on
how to light a small space with a limited budget & fixtures, and on how to use
QLab & ETC.

The lighting design embraced simplicity, with much of it serving as a proof of
concept for future productions. Many of the lighting choices were deliberate
experiments. For example, we had 2 Showtec Compact Pars to add a bit of colour.
Looking back and getting feedback from the audience, I realized the pars were a
bit much, and not needed.

On the hardware side of things, we had a DreyTek router that both QLab & ETC were
connected to, then the lights were sent to a Chauvet DJ sACN Node & converted to
dmx. After that, the dmx cable was connected to a portable dimmer rack, and to
the pars, where they were terminated. For the main lights, we used some Par 16s
(aka Birdies) with barn doors and 3000K bulbs. When I turned on the lights for
the first time, we were genuinely surprised that they worked so well.
